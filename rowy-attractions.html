Active Holiday

Rowy is surrounded by great forests so there are plenty of pathways to walk or ride your bikes. If you don't have bikes with you you can rent them in one of the many vendors across the village. An additional attraction is the Słowiński National Park, which can be toured on foot and by bike.

Słowiński National Park

In the past, the park's area was a Baltic Sea bay. The sea's activity, however, created sand dunes which in the course of time separated the bay from the Baltic Sea. As waves and wind carry sand inland the dunes slowly move, at a speed of 3 to 10 metres per year. Some dunes are quite high - up to 30 metres. The highest peak of the park – Rowokol (115 metres (377 ft) above sea level) – is also an excellent observation point. The "moving dunes" are regarded as a curiosity of nature on a European scale.

Events plenty

There are many interesting events organised in Rowy. They start at the beginning of June and finish up around mid September. Everyone can find something for themselves no matter their age. There are concerts, stand-ups, outdoor movie screenings, dancings, competitions and even a National Mascot Festival! Apart from special events, there are many shops, arcades and theme parks for all to enjoy.

Fresh Seafood

Rowy has always been a fishing village and this tradition is still cultivated today. Every tourist has a chance to try freshly caught fish prepared by some of the best cooks in the Pomeranian Voivodship. You will fall in love with the local Boatswain Soup or the delicious freshly fried cod.
